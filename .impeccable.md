## Design Context

### Users
VCE (Victorian Certificate of Education) students managing coursework during study sessions. They're stressed, time-poor, and need a tool that stays out of their way — fast to navigate, clear to read, no visual clutter. Using this during focused study blocks, not casual browsing.

### Brand Personality
Precise, Minimal, Utilitarian — the app should feel like a well-designed drafting tool: a mechanical pencil on grid paper, a Muji notebook. It earns trust through precision and restraint, not personality or flash.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Precision tool aesthetic, Muji-meets-drafting-table. Clean, neutral, architectural.
- **Theme**: Both dark and light modes must feel equally considered. The dark mode should be comfortable for late-night study sessions; light mode for daytime use.
- **Typography**: Single sans-serif family (Sora) with clear weight hierarchy. Clean geometric character fits the precision tool feel.
- **Color**: Single blue accent used sparingly. Neutrals tinted toward the brand's cool-blue hue for subconscious cohesion.
- **Motion**: Minimal and purposeful — no decorative animations. Transitions should serve a functional purpose.
- **Anti-references**: Not a dashboard, not a social app, not gamified. Avoid the AI-design patterns (gradient text, side-stripes, glassmorphism).

### Design Principles
1. **Fast and tool-like** — every interaction should feel immediate. The app is a tool, not a destination.
2. **Precision over decoration** — spacing, alignment, and typography carry the design. No superfluous visual elements.
3. **Invisible when working** — the interface should fade into the background during study sessions, letting the user focus on their work.
4. **Equally polished in light and dark** — both themes get the same attention to contrast, hierarchy, and refinement.
5. **Accessibility is not optional** — WCAG AA contrast, focus indicators, reduced-motion support. Text must be zoomable to 200%.
